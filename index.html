The Congressional Support for the Use of Military Force (CSUMF) Dataset: Measuring Congressional Sentiment Toward Military Intervention Decisions

Welcome to the website presenting the Congressional Support for the Use of Military Force (CSUMF) dataset. This dataset is a groundbreaking tool for researchers in political science, history, and law who seek to understand the role of congressional sentiment in the ultimate decision-making process of the executive branch regarding military interventions. The CSUMF dataset utilizes floor speeches from the congressional record to measure sentiment toward the use of force in nearly two hundred crises from the end of the Second World War until today. The dataset not only provides an overall congressional sentiment score for each crisis but also breaks down sentiment by political party and allows for dynamic scores, capturing variance within a crisis over time. The validation tests comparing the CSUMF scores to roll call votes and public opinion polls suggest that the CSUMF dataset is a superior proxy for measuring congressional sentiment than any other alternative. This website is a resource for researchers who wish to learn more about the dataset and access its contents.
